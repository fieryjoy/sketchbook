Part I. Grandfathers

My grandfathers side by side. The one on the left is the father of my father and the one on the right is the father of my mother. They both fought in the Second World War and had very vivid memories from those times. Both of them knew how to sew pelts. Both liked to drink and both loved their grandchildren. One was poor and the other one rich. One was slim, the other one fat. One would tell us children stories, the other would give us money. One would smoke, the other one not. They died shortly one after the other.
Figure 1 Grandparents

Part VI. Summer

My mother, my grandmother, my brother and me as a little girl in the summer time. Every summer holidays we would go for at least a few days to the sea side. My brother would swim as far as he could and I would build sand castels while waiting for him. We would get sometimes blue from cold but we would continue staying close to water. Staying dry was never in our focus.
Figure 6 Summer

Part XIX. Autoportret

This is one of the first autopotrets I made. I like the flirtatious look in my eyes and the background dynamics. It’s a fast drawing. I prefer fast drawing because it doesn’t give me time to overthink. This is one of the problems I confront myself with in real life. Overthinking can make your life hard because it blocks your abilities to act and take decisions.
